Artificial Sensory Neurons

Understanding brain complexity through simple technology

At NTNU, we have developed an innovative learning platform that gives pupils and students insight into the fascinating functions of the nervous system. Our artificial neurons make it possible to visualize and understand the basic principles behind human thoughts, feelings, and actions. This physical and digital learning resource is designed to be user-friendly, flexible, and scalable, allowing teachers to adapt their teaching to students' needs and interests.

Why artificial neurons?

The nervous system is a complex world of nerve cells, signals, and synapses that are usually invisible and difficult to explain. Through our simplified models, students can gain an understanding of how the nervous system works in practice, focusing on important reflexes and signal pathways, such as the knee reflex and vestibulo-ocular reflex. This understanding provides a solid foundation for further learning in subjects such as biology, neuroscience, medicine, psychology, and more.

The use of artificial neurons as a teaching tool provides unique advantages in learning situations by combining physical interaction, student activity, and interactive exploration. Research shows that physical models in teaching can contribute to better understanding of abstract concepts, especially in complex fields like neuroscience, where concepts can be difficult to visualize. The artificial neurons activate students through hands-on experience, contributing to a deeper and more lasting understanding. Interactive learning promotes active participation, where students explore, test hypotheses, and directly see how changes in a model affect the entire system. This aligns with research emphasizing the importance of active learning and experimentation, both of which strengthen students' ability to transfer knowledge to new contexts and problems. Artificial neurons as a physical and interactive tool therefore engage students in a holistic way, making complex topics more accessible and inspiring to explore.
